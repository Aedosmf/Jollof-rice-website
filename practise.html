About Jollof Rice

Jollof Rice is a popular and iconic dish in Nigeria and many other West African countries. It is a one-pot rice dish made with a rich tomato sauce, rice, and various spices. It is often served at celebrations, parties, and family gatherings.

Jollof Rice Recipe

  • 2 cups of rice
  • 3 large tomatoes (blended)
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 1 red bell pepper (blended)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cloves of garlic (minced)
  • 1 bay leaf

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat oil and saut√© onions and garlic until soft.
  2. Add the blended tomatoes, red bell pepper, and spices. Stir well and cook until the sauce thickens.
  3. Add the rice and mix thoroughly with the tomato sauce.
  4. Pour in the chicken broth, cover, and cook on low heat until the rice is tender.
  5. Serve with grilled or fried chicken, fish, or any preferred side dish.

History of Jollof Rice

Jollof Rice is said to have originated from the Senegambia region in West Africa. The dish is widely enjoyed across various African countries, with each country having its own version. In Nigeria, it has become a staple for special occasions, especially during weddings, holidays, and celebrations.
